What is Enchanting?

Enchanting allows you to add powerful stat bonuses to your equipment using Pixels and Crystals. On each item type you have 2 unique enchantments to pick from - choose wisely. This equipment enhancement system provides guaranteed magical bonuses with 100% success rate.

How Enchanting Works

There are three enchanting actions you can perform:

  • New Enchant: Add an enchantment to an item for the first time (level 0 â†’ level 1)
  • Upgrade Enchant: Increase an existing enchantment by one level (level 1 â†’ level 2)
  • Change Enchant: Replace an existing enchantment with a different one (Cheaper and keeps the same level!)
Guaranteed Success: Enchanting has a 100% success rate - no risk of failure!
Level Preservation: When you switch enchantments, the new enchantment inherits the level from the old one! For example, if you have Deadly Strike level 2 and switch to Piercing, you'll get Piercing level 2 immediately. This makes experimenting with different enchantments much less costly!

Crystals

Crystals can be obtained by defeating enemies, but the fastest way to farm crystals is in the mine! Crystal farming in mining areas provides the most efficient resource gathering for enchanting.

Enchantment Types

Enchantments are restricted by equipment type. Each item slot has access to exactly two specific enchantments - you cannot apply any other enchantment types to that item. This ensures each equipment piece has unique enhancement options.

For example: Weapons can ONLY have Deadly Strike or Piercing. You cannot add Life Steal or any other enchantment to a weapon. This slot-specific enchanting system creates strategic choices for gear optimization.

Enchanting Costs

Enchanting requires both Pixels and Crystals. Costs scale based on item quality, item level, rarity, item type, and enchantment level.

What Affects Costs?

Enchanting costs increase with:

  • Higher Item Rarity: Legendary and Artifact items cost more to enchant than Common items
  • Higher Item Level: Level 50 items cost more than level 10 items
  • Better Item Quality: Items with higher quality percentage cost more
  • Higher Enchantment Level: Upgrading to level 2 costs more than the initial level 1 enchant
  • Item Type: Different equipment slots have different base costs
Resource Management: Focus on enchanting your best gear first. You can always change enchantments later for a low cost if you want to experiment with different builds.

FAQ

What is enchanting in Idle Adventure?

Enchanting allows you to add magical stat bonuses to equipment using Pixels and Crystals. Each item slot has access to exactly two specific enchantments. Enchanting has 100% success rate and enchantments can be upgraded to level 2 for increased effectiveness.

What enchantments are available for each equipment slot?

Each slot has two enchantments: Weapons have Deadly Strike or Piercing, Armor has Endurance or Blocking, Helm has Life Steal or Consume Souls, Legs have Mana Absorb or Mana Leech, Boots have Swiftness or Reflect, and Gloves have Open Wounds or Crushing Blows.

Can I change enchantments without losing levels?

Yes! When you switch enchantments, the new enchantment inherits the level from the old one. If you have a level 2 enchantment and switch to a different one, you'll get the new enchantment at level 2 immediately. Changing enchantments is also cheaper than adding new ones.

What currencies do enchantments require?

Enchanting requires Pixels and Crystals. Costs scale based on item quality, item level, rarity, and enchantment level. Changing enchantments is the cheapest option, making it cost-effective to experiment with different bonuses.
