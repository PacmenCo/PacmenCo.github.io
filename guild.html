Guild Boss Events

Guild bosses are time-limited cooperative challenges where all guild members work together to defeat powerful bosses. The entire guild shares a common HP pool that all members damage during their individual runs.

Event Schedule

  • Event Duration: 108 hours (4.5 days)
  • Cooldown Between Bosses: 9.5 hours
  • Total Cycle: ~117.5 hours (~5 days per boss rotation)
  • Boss Rotation: Boss 1 ‚Üí Boss 2 ‚Üí Boss 3 ‚Üí Boss 4 ‚Üí Repeat

Boss HP Pools

Each boss type has a different shared HP pool that the entire guild damages together:

  • Boss 1: 1,000,000 HP (shared)
  • Boss 2: 2,000,000 HP (shared)
  • Boss 3: 3,000,000 HP (shared)
  • Boss 4: 1,500,000 HP (shared)

Ticket System

Players use tickets to participate in guild boss runs. Each run lasts 2 minutes, during which you fight the boss to deal as much damage as possible.

Ticket Mechanics

  • Maximum Tickets: 3 per player
  • Regeneration Time: 8.5 hours per ticket
  • New Member Starting Tickets: 1 ticket
  • Run Duration: 120 seconds (2 minutes)

Tickets regenerate automatically over time. When you have less than 3 tickets, the UI displays a countdown timer showing when your next ticket will be available. You cannot start a run without at least 1 ticket.

Boss Difficulty & Progression

Guild bosses feature a dynamic difficulty system where each time you defeat the boss (deplete one HP bar), the difficulty increases and the boss becomes stronger with a refilled HP bar.

Starting Difficulty

Starting Difficulty = max(1, Last Reached Difficulty - 3)

The game remembers the highest difficulty you've reached. Your next run always starts 3 levels below your best, with a minimum of difficulty level 1. For example, if you previously reached difficulty 10, your next run starts at difficulty 7.

Damage Reduction

Starting at difficulty 6, bosses gain additional damage reduction:

Damage Reduction = 2% per difficulty level above 5 (capped at 99%)
Difficulty Display: The UI shows difficulty as 0-indexed (internal difficulty 1 displays as "Difficulty 0").

Valor (Player Currency)

Valor is the personal currency earned from guild boss participation. It can be spent in the Valor Shop on companions, skills, and powerful guild buffs.

Earning Valor

Valor = (Guild HP Removed √∑ 5%) + Kill Bonus
  • 1 Valor Point per 5% of boss HP removed by your guild
  • 5 Bonus Valor Points if the boss is killed (100% HP removed)
  • Maximum: 25 Valor (20 from damage + 5 bonus)

Valor Examples

  • 100% HP removed (boss killed): 25 Valor (20 + 5 bonus)
  • 95% HP removed: 19 Valor
  • 50% HP removed: 10 Valor
  • 25% HP removed: 5 Valor
  • No participation: 0 Valor
Important: Rewards are identical for all participants! Whether you dealt 1 damage or 1 million damage, everyone who attacked receives the same Valor based on the guild's total performance.

Claiming Rewards

Valor rewards are not automatic. You must:

  • Navigate to the Guild Boss Rewards panel after the event
  • Click the "Claim!" button
  • Valor is added to your spendable balance

Valor Shop

Spend your Valor on:

  • Companions: Permanent unlocks that fight alongside you
  • Skills: Permanent new abilities for your character
  • Guild Buffs: Temporary consumable buffs that benefit your entire guild

Guild Buff Types

  • Attack Bonus - Increased damage output
  • Defense Bonus - Reduced damage taken
  • XP Bonus - Faster leveling
  • Loot Bonus - Better drop rates
  • Health Bonus - Increased max HP
  • Mana Bonus - Increased max mana
  • Companion Speed & Range - Faster and longer-range companion attacks
  • Companion Damage & Stamina - More powerful companions with higher stamina
  • Companion XP Bonus - Faster companion leveling
  • Guild Boss Killer - Extra damage specifically against guild bosses

Sigils (Guild Currency)

Sigils are the guild-wide currency earned from boss participation. While Valor is personal, Sigils belong to the entire guild and are likely used for guild upgrades and features.

Strategy Tips

Maximize Your Contribution

  • Start your run at a difficulty you can handle - going too high means less time actually damaging the boss
  • Watch for the damage cap enrage (2% of remaining HP) and stop before triggering it
  • Coordinate with guild members to avoid wasting tickets during low-activity periods
  • Save tickets for when the boss is fresh and easier to damage

Managing Enrage

  • Difficulty enrage is inevitable after 6 difficulties - plan your build to survive it
  • Damage cap enrage can be avoided by monitoring your total damage output
  • If both enrages trigger, focus on survival over damage

Valor Shop Priorities

  • Unlock essential companions first - they provide permanent value
  • Save Valor for key skills that complement your build
  • Guild buffs are consumable - coordinate with your guild before purchasing
