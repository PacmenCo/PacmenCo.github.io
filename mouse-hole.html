Overview

The Mouse Hole Raid is a unique timed challenge located in The Cottage (House). Entry requires a Saw. Using a Saw opens a 6 minute 30 second window where you can complete multiple combat runs and open treasure chests. The raid features two distinct zones and culminates in a score-scaled Magic Ring with Speed% bonuses.

  • Location: The Cottage (House)
  • Entry Requirement: 1 Saw per window (6m 30s)
  • Unlock: Level 125
  • Excellent Orb Farm: 25% from treasure chests & 4% from each Mouse
  • Unique Reward: Score-scaled Magic Ring with Speed% bonuses
  • Special rules: You do not lose rank experience on death (You cant die in the Mouse Hole!)

Access Window

Using 1 Saw opens the Mouse Hole for exactly 6 minutes and 30 seconds. During this time window, you have limited interactions:

  • Treasure Chest Limit: Maximum 2 chest openings during the window
  • Combat Run Limit: Maximum 4 total raid completions during the window
  • Window Duration: 6m30s, decreases by 30s per passive reward tick
  • Multiple Entries: Can re-enter multiple times until limits are hit or timer expires

Timing Strategy: Each combat run takes 5 minutes. You can realistically fit 1-2 runs within the 6m30s window before you need to use another saw!

Special Raid Rules

The Mouse Hole has unique combat mechanics that drastically change how your stats work:

  • No XP Loss on Death: You cannot lose rank experience - death teleports you to the treasure room
  • Modified Health: Base HP reduced to 5%, but you gain +10,000 flat health
  • Modified Attack: Power from items reduced to 5% + 10 flat damage
  • Modified Armor: Armor from items reduced to 5% + 100 flat armor
  • Melee Bonus: Melee weapons deal 1.5x damage (ranged weapons have no bonus)
  • Enhanced Crits: 25% base crit chance, 3.0x base crit multiplier (crit damage bonuses 50% effective)
  • High HP Regen: 15% of normal regen + 1,800 flat regen
  • Weakened Pets: Pet damage reduced to 10% + 5 flat damage
  • Reduced Resist: Resistance reduced to 20% of normal

Build Impact: These special rules create a completely different meta. Up to you to adapt your build for maximum efficiency!

Saw Crafting

Saws are crafted using Pixels and Voxels. The cost increases with each saw crafted but also decays over time.

Penalty System

  • Penalty per purchase: 154 penalty per saw crafted
  • Result: Slowest decay of all special areas - costs stay elevated longer

Decay per tick: 1 + penalty/175
Applied every 30 seconds

Decay Examples

  • At 175 penalty: Decay = 1 + (175/175) = 2 per tick
  • At 350 penalty: Decay = 1 + (350/175) = 3 per tick
  • At 700 penalty: Decay = 1 + (700/175) = 5 per tick

Conclusion: Higher penalties decay significantly faster. Mouse Hole saws decay slower than other special area keys (divider of 175 vs 115 for Cave and 80 for Tower), meaning costs take longer to normalize.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I unlock The Mouse Hole Raid?
The Mouse Hole Raid unlocks at level 125. It is located in The Cottage (House). You need to craft Saws using Pixels and Voxels to enter the raid. Each Saw opens a 6 minute 30 second window where you can complete multiple combat runs and open treasure chests.
What are the access window limits?
Using 1 Saw opens a 6m30s window. During this time, you can open a maximum of 2 treasure chests and complete a maximum of 4 combat runs. Each combat run takes 5 minutes, so you can realistically fit 1-2 runs within the window. You can re-enter multiple times until limits are hit or the timer expires.
How does the Magic Ring scaling work?
The Magic Ring has a score-scaled Speed% bonus calculated as: Speed% = Log10(1 + score/1600) √ó 1.77 + Random(0.3 to 1.0). Higher scores grant better rings. The logarithmic scaling means doubling your score doesn't double your Speed%. Focus on consistent high scores for optimal ring quality with random enchantments.
What's the best strategy for orb farming in The Mouse Hole?
Zone 1 treasure chests have a 25% Orb drop rate (6x higher than Zone 0's 4% rate from mice). Prioritize opening both chests every run for maximum Orb gains. The Mouse Hole is an excellent location for random Orb farming due to these high drop rates combined with consistent combat encounters.
Why don't I lose rank experience when I die in The Mouse Hole?
This is because death is the intended way to reach the treasure room - dying teleports you directly there to claim your rewards. Since dying is mandatory, losing XP would be frustrating and make the dungeon almost pointless to play.
