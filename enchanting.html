✨ Enchanting & Alteration

Two powerful systems to upgrade and customize your equipment

What is Enchanting?

Enchanting allows you to add powerful stat bonuses to your equipment using Pixels (coins) and Crystals (enchanting materials). Each item can have up to level 2 enchantments, with different enchantment types available based on the item slot.

How Enchanting Works

There are three enchanting actions you can perform:

  • New Enchant: Add an enchantment to an item for the first time (level 0 → level 1)
  • Upgrade Enchant: Increase an existing enchantment by one level (level 1 → level 2)
  • Change Enchant: Replace an existing enchantment with a different one (keeps the same level!)
Guaranteed Success: Enchanting has a 100% success rate - there is no chance of failure!
Level Preservation: When you switch from one enchantment to another, the new enchantment inherits the level from the old one! For example, if you have Deadly Strike level 2 and switch to Piercing, you'll get Piercing level 2 immediately. This makes experimenting with different enchantments much less costly!

Enchanting Materials

Crystals (enchanting materials) are obtained by defeating enemies. The amount dropped is based on:

  • Enemy level (higher level enemies drop more)
  • Level difference between you and the enemy (fighting enemies 8+ levels below you reduces drops)
  • Random variation in loot rolls
Base Amount = 0-2 + 0-2 + Random(1, max_roll+1)
Where: max_roll = (int)(2.76 × (enemyLevel/levelsPerRealLevel + 15) / 20)

If you are 8+ levels above the enemy, Crystal drops are reduced by the formula:

Reduction = (levelDiff^0.9 + 34) / (levelDiff^0.9 + 14) - 1
(Minimum 55% reduction)

Enchantment Types

Enchantments are restricted by equipment type. Each item slot has access to exactly two specific enchantments - you cannot apply any other enchantment types to that item.

For example, weapons can ONLY have Deadly Strike or Piercing. You cannot add Life Steal or any other enchantment to a weapon.

Enchanting Costs

Enchanting requires both Pixels (coins) and Crystals (enchanting materials). Costs scale based on item quality, item type, and enchantment level.

Pixel Cost Formula

For New/Upgrade Enchant:
Cost = (int)(itemCost × itemQuality × (enchantLevel + 1)) × itemTypeMultiplier

For Change Enchant:
Cost = (int)(itemCost/2 × itemQuality + 5) × (1 + 2×enchantLevel)

Note on Change Enchant: The cost uses the current enchantment level in the formula because the new enchantment will keep the same level. This means changing a level 2 enchantment costs more than changing a level 1 enchantment, but you get to keep the level 2 on the new enchant!

What is Item Upgrading?

Item Upgrading is a system that allows you to permanently increase an item's base stats using Pixels (coins) and Voxels (crystals). Each upgrade level increases the item's damage or armor by 5%, making it significantly more powerful.

How Upgrading Works

Each item has an itemStrength value that represents its upgrade level. When you upgrade an item:

  • The item's upgrade level (itemStrength) increases by 1
  • Damage or armor increases by 5% of the base value
  • Enchantments are preserved during upgrades
  • Cost increases exponentially with each level
Guaranteed Success: Item upgrades have a 100% success rate. Your enchantments and item properties are always preserved!

Stat Scaling Formula

Final Stat = Base Stat × Item Quality × (1 + itemStrength × 0.05)

Example:
Base Damage: 100
Item Quality: 1.5 (Epic)
Upgrade Level: 4
Final Damage = 100 × 1.5 × (1 + 4 × 0.05) = 100 × 1.5 × 1.2 = 180

Each upgrade level adds +5% multiplicative bonus. At upgrade level 10, your item deals +50% more damage/armor!

Item Upgrade Costs

Upgrade costs scale exponentially with upgrade level, item quality, and item rarity. Higher quality and rarer items cost significantly more to upgrade.

Pixel Cost Formula

Cost = itemTypeMultiplier × itemCost × 0.34 ×
1.9^((itemQuality/1.25 + 0.1)^1.6 + itemStrength/1.75) × (2 × itemQuality - 1.97)

The formula uses several constants to create exponential growth:

  • a = 0.34 (base cost multiplier)
  • b = 1.9 (exponential base)
  • c = 1.6 (quality exponent)
  • d = -0.1 (quality offset)
  • e = 1.75 (strength divisor)
  • f = 1.25 (quality divisor)

Voxel Cost Formula

ilvlScale = (itemLevel + 10) / 50 × 5

Cost = ilvlScale × itemTypeMultiplier ×
((rarity - 1)^2 + 0.4 × itemStrength^1.55 × (2 × rarity - 3))^1.35

Voxel costs increase based on:

  • Item level (higher level = more voxels)
  • Item rarity (exponential scaling)
  • Current upgrade level (power of 1.55)
  • Item type (weapon vs armor multipliers)

Max Upgrade Level

The maximum upgrade level you can achieve increases with your player level and ascension bonuses.

Max Upgrade Level = 1 + AscensionEnchantingModifier + playerLevel / (15 × levelsPerRealLevel)

This means:

  • Base max level: 1
  • Ascension skills can increase the cap (+1 from Enchanting skill)
  • Every 75 actual levels grants +1 max upgrade level
  • Post-prestige players use max level (301) instead of actual level

Upgrading vs Enchanting vs Alteration

Understanding the differences between these three systems helps you choose the best path for improving your equipment.

When to Use Each System

  • Item Upgrading: Your primary method of increasing power. Always upgrade your best equipment first.
  • Enchanting: Add after upgrading to complement your build (damage, defense, utility).
  • Item Alteration: Use for fine-tuning high-tier items or fixing unwanted affixes.
Pro Tip: Upgrade your item first, then enchant it, and finally use alteration orbs for optimization. This order minimizes wasted resources if you replace the item later.

What is Item Alteration?

Item Alteration is an advanced crafting system that allows you to modify item properties using Alteration Orbs. Unlike enchanting, alteration can reroll stats, add/remove affixes, upgrade rarity, and improve item quality.

High Risk, High Reward: Some alteration types can break your item, reducing it to Broken rarity. Use with caution on valuable equipment!

Affix System

Items can have 0-3 affixes maximum. Affixes are additional stat modifiers that enhance your equipment beyond base stats and enchantments.

Affix Rules

  • Maximum: 3 affixes per item
  • Reroll One Affix: Requires at least 1 affix, max 2 affixes on item
  • Reroll All Affixes: Requires at least 1 affix, max 3 affixes on item
  • Remove Random Affix: Requires at least 1 affix to remove
  • Add New Affix: Can add if item has fewer than 3 affixes
  • Fill With Affixes: Requires exactly 2 affixes, adds 1-3 more
Fill With Affixes Distribution:
- 65% chance: Add 1 affix
- 31% chance: Add 2 affixes
- 4% chance: Add 3 affixes

Quality & Rarity Upgrades

Alteration orbs can improve item quality and rarity, but some operations carry significant risk.

Orb of Fortune (Reroll Quality/Rarity)

  • 5% chance: Increase rarity by 1 tier
  • 50% chance: Item breaks (becomes Broken rarity)
  • 45% chance: No change
  • Cannot be used on Artifact items
High Risk: Orb of Fortune has a 50% chance to break your item! Only use on items you're willing to lose.

Soul Level System

Soul levels are a special item property that allows equipment to gain experience and level up over time.

How Soul Levels Work

  • Items can have 0-5 soul levels maximum
  • Use Soulstones to add +1 max soul level per use
  • Only items with soul levels can gain experience and level up
  • Leveling items can grant additional affixes and stat bonuses
  • Soulstones are the rarest orb type (0.1% drop rate)
Pro Tip: Reserve Soulstones for your best Legendary or Artifact items to maximize their long-term potential.

Strategy Tips

Enchanting Strategy

  • Enchant your best items first - enchantments are permanent until changed
  • Choose enchantments that complement your build (damage for offense, endurance for defense)
  • Weapons and armor are expensive to enchant - save crystals for your best gear
  • Max enchant level increases with player level - wait for higher levels for better returns

Alteration Strategy

  • Use cheap alterations (Remove/Reroll Affix) on common items to experiment
  • Save expensive alterations (Fill With Affixes, Upgrade Rarity) for rare items
  • Never use Orb of Fortune on items you can't afford to lose
  • Upgrade quality with Armor Plates/Sharpening Stones early - no risk and permanent benefit
  • Soulstones are extremely rare - use them only on Legendary/Artifact items you plan to keep
  • Artifact items cost 20 orbs per alteration - alteration is prohibitively expensive
